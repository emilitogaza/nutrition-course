# Dietary Fat & Hormones

Fat spent decades as the villain, then carbs took the role. Neither framing was
right. Dietary fat is **essential** — literally, in the case of two fatty acids —
and it plays a direct role in hormone production, nutrient absorption, and cell
structure. The practical questions are *how much* and *which kinds*.

## Why you can't go too low

Fat isn't just a fuel. It:

- Builds every **cell membrane** in the body.
- Carries the **fat-soluble vitamins** A, D, E, and K — eat these with too little
  fat and you absorb less of them.
- Supplies cholesterol as the backbone for **steroid hormones**, including
  **testosterone** and oestrogen.

Two fats are **essential** because the body can't synthesise them:
**omega-6 (linoleic acid)** and **omega-3 (alpha-linolenic acid)**.

### The fat–testosterone link

Very low-fat diets are associated with **lower testosterone**. A 2021
meta-analysis found that men who dropped to low-fat diets saw meaningful
reductions in total and free testosterone versus higher-fat intakes. The effect
is real but dose-dependent — it shows up mainly at the extremes (roughly < 20% of
calories from fat). For most people, keeping fat at a sensible floor avoids the
problem entirely.

> **Practical floor.** Keep fat at least **~0.5–1 g/kg body weight**, or roughly
> **20–35% of total calories**. That's enough for hormones and vitamin absorption
> with room to flex carbs around it.

## The types of fat

| Type | Found in | Verdict |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Monounsaturated** | Olive oil, avocado, nuts | Robustly healthy |
| **Polyunsaturated (omega-3)** | Fatty fish, flax, walnuts, chia | Beneficial; most people under-consume |
| **Polyunsaturated (omega-6)** | Vegetable/seed oils, nuts | Essential; usually over-consumed relative to omega-3 |
| **Saturated** | Meat, dairy, coconut, palm | Fine in moderation; context matters |
| **Trans (industrial)** | Partially hydrogenated oils | Avoid — the only truly "bad" fat |

Industrial **trans fats** are the one category with no defensible role — they're
now banned or restricted in many countries. Everything else is a matter of
proportion.

## Omega-3s: the fat most worth attention

Omega-3s — specifically **EPA and DHA** from fatty fish — are anti-inflammatory,
support cardiovascular and brain health, and may aid recovery and muscle
protein synthesis in some populations (notably older adults). Most people eating
a Western diet get **plenty of omega-6 and too little omega-3**, skewing the
ratio.

- **Food first:** 2–3 servings of fatty fish per week (salmon, mackerel,
  sardines, herring) covers most people.
- **Supplement** (fish oil or algae-based for vegans) is reasonable if you don't
  eat fish — a common target is ~**1–3 g combined EPA+DHA per day**. Note that
  plant ALA (flax, chia) converts to EPA/DHA only poorly, so it's not a full
  substitute.

## The takeaway

- Fat is **essential** for hormones, membranes, and vitamin absorption — don't
  drive it too low.
- Keep it at **~0.5–1 g/kg** (≈ 20–35% of calories); very low-fat can suppress
  **testosterone**.
- Favour **mono- and polyunsaturated** fats; avoid **industrial trans fats**.
- Prioritise **omega-3 (EPA/DHA)** — most people don't get enough.
