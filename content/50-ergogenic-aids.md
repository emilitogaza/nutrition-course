# Ergogenic Aids That Work

Out of the thousands of supplements sold on performance claims, only a handful
survive rigorous testing. The Australian Institute of Sport's **"Group A"** — its
top evidence tier — and the IOC consensus statement both land on roughly the same
short list. If a supplement isn't here, the bar for spending money on it should be
high.

## Creatine — the most proven of all

**Creatine monohydrate** is the most studied, most effective, and cheapest sports
supplement in existence. It works by topping up **phosphocreatine** in muscle,
which regenerates ATP during short, intense efforts — so you can do a bit more
work, and that extra work drives more adaptation over time.

- **Benefits:** more strength and power, greater training volume, more lean mass
  over months, and — increasingly studied — possible **cognitive** benefits under
  stress or sleep deprivation.
- **Dose:** **3–5 g/day**, every day. A "loading phase" of ~20 g/day (split into
  4 doses) for 5–7 days fills stores faster but isn't necessary — steady 5 g gets
  you there in ~3–4 weeks.
- **Form:** plain **monohydrate**. The fancier, pricier forms (HCl, buffered,
  "Kre-Alkalyn") offer no proven advantage.
- **Notes:** the initial ~1–2 kg gain is **water inside muscle**, not fat. It's
  safe long-term in healthy people; it does **not** harm kidneys. Works whether
  taken pre- or post-workout — consistency matters more than timing.

## Caffeine — reliable across almost everything

Caffeine is a genuine, broad-spectrum ergogenic — it improves endurance, power,
sprint performance, reaction time, and perceived effort by acting on the central
nervous system (blocking adenosine).

- **Dose:** **3–6 mg/kg** body weight, ~**45–60 minutes** before exercise. More
  isn't better and raises side effects.
- **Notes:** effects vary with genetics (caffeine-metabolism genes) and habitual
  intake. Cycling off periodically may restore sensitivity. Watch the interaction
  with sleep — caffeine has a ~5-hour half-life, so afternoon doses cost you at
  night.

## Beta-alanine — for the 1–4 minute burn

Beta-alanine raises muscle **carnosine**, which buffers the acidity that builds up
during sustained high-intensity effort. The benefit is specific: efforts lasting
roughly **1–4 minutes** (think 400–1500 m, rowing, high-rep sets, repeated
sprints).

- **Dose:** **3.2–6.4 g/day**, taken **daily** for weeks — it works by
  *saturating* muscle carnosine, not acutely. Split doses to reduce the harmless
  **tingling (paraesthesia)**.

## Dietary nitrate / beetroot — endurance economy

Nitrate (from **beetroot juice** or leafy greens) converts to nitric oxide,
improving blood flow and making muscle contractions more **oxygen-efficient**.

- **Benefit:** improved endurance and efficiency, most useful for recreational-to-
  trained athletes (elite responders vary).
- **Dose:** ~**6–13 mmol (≈ 300–600 mg) nitrate**, ~2–3 hours before, or loaded
  over several days. Avoid antibacterial mouthwash — it kills the oral bacteria
  that convert nitrate.

## Sodium bicarbonate — the buffer, from outside the cell

Plain **baking soda** raises blood bicarbonate, buffering acid from
high-intensity work — complementary to beta-alanine (which buffers *inside* the
cell).

- **Dose:** ~**0.2–0.3 g/kg**, ~60–150 min before. The catch is **GI distress**;
  newer enteric-coated capsules and split dosing reduce it. Trial it in training,
  never first on race day.

## A note for tested athletes

Even proven supplements carry a **contamination risk** — products spiked or
cross-contaminated with banned substances cause failed drug tests every year.
Anyone subject to testing should use only products with **third-party
certification** (Informed Sport, NSF Certified for Sport).

## The takeaway

| Supplement | Best for | Typical dose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Creatine monohydrate** | Strength, power, muscle | 3–5 g/day |
| **Caffeine** | Almost everything | 3–6 mg/kg pre-workout |
| **Beta-alanine** | 1–4 min high-intensity | 3.2–6.4 g/day (chronic) |
| **Nitrate / beetroot** | Endurance economy | ~300–600 mg, 2–3 h pre |
| **Bicarbonate** | High-intensity buffering | 0.2–0.3 g/kg pre |

Creatine and caffeine are the two nearly everyone can benefit from; the rest are
**sport-specific** tools.
