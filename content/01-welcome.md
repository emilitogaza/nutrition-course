# Welcome to Fuel Lab

This is a course about **nutritional science** — the study of how what you eat
changes your body. The lens is performance and body composition: how to fuel
training, recover faster, build and keep muscle, and lose fat without wrecking
your metabolism or your sanity. Everything here applies just as well if your
goal is simply to look and feel better.

It's written to **start light and go deep**. The early chapters assume nothing.
The later ones get into per-meal leucine thresholds, DIAAS scores, and where the
research is still genuinely unsettled. Skip around — or read straight through.

## What you'll learn

- How energy balance actually works, and why "calories in, calories out" is both
  true and incomplete
- How much protein you really need, and why *amino acids* — not just grams — are
  the thing that matters
- What carbohydrates and fats do for performance and hormones — and where alcohol
  fits
- Which vitamins and minerals matter, when food beats supplements, and when it
  doesn't
- The short list of supplements with real evidence — and the long list without
- How to lose fat while holding onto muscle — and how to build muscle without
  piling on fat
- How to time and structure food around training, and why sleep and recovery are
  body-composition variables too
- Where the general advice needs adjusting — for women, older, and young athletes
- Where the frontier is: the gut microbiome, meal timing, and open questions

## How to read the evidence

Nutrition research is noisy. Studies are short, samples are small, and headlines
outrun the data constantly. A few habits worth keeping as you read:

- **Dose and context matter more than the nutrient.** "Protein is good" tells you
  nothing; *how much, from what, when, for whom* is the whole question.
- **Trained and untrained bodies respond differently.** A beginner grows on
  almost anything; an advanced athlete needs precision.
- **Effect sizes beat significance.** Something can be "statistically proven" and
  still be too small to matter in your life.
